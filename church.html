Church History

Tracing the roots of the Christian faith from the Cross to the modern day.

1st Century

The Apostolic Age

Following the resurrection and ascension of Jesus Christ, the Holy Spirit descended at Pentecost. The Apostles, led by Peter and later Paul, began spreading the Gospel beyond Jerusalem to the Gentile world, establishing the very first churches.

2nd - 3rd Century

The Age of Martyrs

The early church faced fierce persecution under the Roman Empire. Despite the threat of death in arenas, the faith grew rapidly. Early Church Fathers and Apologists arose to defend the true Gospel against heresies.

4th Century

The Imperial Church

Emperor Constantine legalized Christianity in 313 AD. The Council of Nicaea (325 AD) was convened to formalize the doctrine of the Holy Trinity, giving us the Nicene Creed which defends the divinity of Jesus Christ.

11th Century

The Great Schism

Theological and political differences caused the first major split in Christian history in 1054 AD, dividing the church into the Western (Roman Catholic) and Eastern (Orthodox) branches.

16th Century

The Protestant Reformation

Sparked by figures like Martin Luther and John Calvin, the Reformation called the church back to scriptural authority. It emphasized that salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone.

Present Day

The Global Church

Today, the body of Christ spans across every nation and tongue. The mission remains the same: to preach the Gospel of Jesus Christ to the ends of the earth and make disciples of all nations.
